# FTC Disclosure & Promo Code of Conduct (v1)

The FTC requires that any "material connection" between an endorser (you) and a brand be **clearly and conspicuously** disclosed. As a paid Momfluencer, every post you make about a MomFluence offer creates a material connection. **Disclosure is not optional.**

## 1. The minimum

- Use **`#ad`** or **`#sponsored`** in the first line of your caption, before any read-more cutoff.
- On Reels / TikTok, also use the platform's **"Paid partnership"** label.
- On Stories, use a **text sticker** with `#ad`. Don't bury disclosure under five other stickers.

## 2. What does NOT count as disclosure

- `#sp`, `#ambassador`, `#partner` alone — too ambiguous, FTC-rejected.
- Disclosure 30 hashtags deep at the bottom of a caption.
- Disclosure inside a long block of italicized text the reader skims past.
- "Thanks @brand for the freebie" without `#ad`.
- A bio line saying "all my posts may be sponsored." Each post needs its own disclosure.

## 3. Platform-specific rules

| Platform | Required |
|---|---|
| Instagram (feed/reel) | Use **Branded content / Paid partnership** label *and* `#ad` in caption. |
| Instagram Story | Text sticker `#ad`, visible from frame 1. |
| TikTok | Toggle **Branded content** in post settings *and* `#ad` in caption. |
| Facebook | "Paid partnership with [brand]" label. |
| YouTube | Check the "Includes paid promotion" box at upload, *and* mention sponsorship in the first 30 seconds. |
| Pinterest | `#ad` in pin description. |

## 4. Allowed claims

- "I had a great experience at [brand]."
- "My kids love [brand]."
- "Booking was easy."

## 5. Prohibited claims (especially for healthcare offers)

- Diagnostic claims ("treats", "cures", "heals").
- Outcome guarantees ("you will lose X pounds").
- Comparative medical claims ("better than [drug]").
- "FDA approved" unless the brand explicitly is.
- Fake testimonials or fake before/afters.

## 6. If you mess up

Edit or delete the post and email `compliance@momfluence.app` within 24 hours of noticing. Repeat violations result in account suspension; willful violations are immediate cause for termination under Section 9 of the Sub-Affiliate Agreement.
